🎾 Topic Deep Dive — The 4 Doubles Serve Patterns¶

*Serve Trong Đôi¶

Assumptions - Skill level: 3.5 recreational (USTA / NTRP equivalent) - Depth: standard — simple cues, clear drills, no pro-level patterns - Language: EN - Voice: friendly master coach, honest about difficulty, focused on what you can copy this month - The big ideas: (1) In doubles, the serve is a SETUP, not a weapon. (2) The 4 serve patterns are: Wide, T, Body, Serve + Volley. (3) At 50+, the slice serve is the joint-friendly default. - Rational default for 3.5+: The slice serve to the backhand (Wide).** The flat serve, the kick serve, and the body serve are "next steps."

Chapter 1 — The Big Idea: The Serve Is a Setup, Not a Weapon¶

🇺🇸
In singles, the serve IS a weapon. A 200 km/h serve is an ace. A 150 km/h serve is a setup for the next shot.
In doubles, the serve is almost never an ace. In doubles, the serve is a setup.
Why? Because the net partner is waiting to poach. A serve that's "fast enough to ace" is also fast enough to give the net partner a clear interception. The 3.5 doubles serve is not about power — it's about placement and coordination with your partner.
The 3.5 player who tries to "blast" the serve in doubles loses more points than they win. The ball is in the net, or long, or right at the poacher. The smart doubles serve: slice, place, coordinate.

🧠 Mental model Think of the serve in doubles as a chess opening. The serve is your first move. The 4 serve patterns are the 4 most common chess openings in doubles.** Each one leads to a different middle game. The smart player picks the serve pattern that matches the situation AND coordinates with the net partner.

Hình dung serve trong đôi như khai cuộc cờ vua. Serve là nước đi đầu. 4 mẫu serve là 4 khai cuộc phổ biến nhất trong đôi. Mỗi cái dẫn tới trung cuộc khác nhau. Người chơi thông minh chọn mẫu serve phù hợp tình huống VÀ phối hợp với partner lưới.

⚠️ The 3.5 mistake The 3.5 player treats the doubles serve like a singles serve. They hit it as hard as they can, aim at the center, and hope. The ball is too fast for the returner to handle (good), but ALSO too fast for the returner to control (bad — the returner can't return, the poacher can't poach, the ball is in the net). The fix: hit the doubles serve at 70-80% power, with slice, to a specific target. The smart doubles serve is a sliced, placed, coordinated serve — not a power serve.**

Sửa: đánh serve đôi ở 70-80% lực, với slice, vào mục tiêu cụ thể. Serve đôi thông minh là serve slice, đặt, phối hợp — không phải serve lực.**

2 Serve to the T 🇺🇸 Down the middle, makes the returner hit up at the poacher 🇻🇳 Xuôi giữa, ép người trả đánh lên vào người cắt When you want the poach Khi muốn cắt Smart option

🧠 The "first move determines the middle game" principle The 4 serve patterns map to 4 different middle-game situations: - Serve Wide → the returner is pulled wide, the poach covers the middle (the smart 50+ default) - Serve to the T → the returner is jammed in the middle, the poach has 2 sides to cover (the poacher's dream) - Serve to the Body → the returner has no swing, the poach covers the open court - Serve + Volley** → the server becomes the poacher (offensive 50+ option)

4 mẫu serve map tới 4 tình huống trung cuộc khác nhau: - Serve Wide → người trả bị kéo rộng, người cắt che giữa (mặc định thông minh 50+) - Serve T → người trả bị ép giữa, người cắt có 2 bên để che (mơ ước người cắt) - Serve Thân → người trả không vung được, người cắt che sân mở - Serve + Volley → server trở thành người cắt (tấn công 50+)

Pick the pattern that matches the situation. The pattern IS the strategy.

The 50+ Slice Serve — Why Slice Is the Doubles Default¶

🇺🇸
At 50+, the slice serve is the doubles default. Why?
1. Less shoulder load. The slice is a brushing motion, not a flat hit. The shoulder doesn't absorb the impact — the strings do. At 50+, the slice is the joint-friendly serve.
2. Lower trajectory. The slice dips through the contact zone, then curves DOWN. The ball lands at the receiver's feet, not at their shoulders. Lower target = more margin for error.
3. Curves away from the returner. The slice curves to the LEFT (for right-handers serving to a right-handed returner). The returner has to reach across their body. Curve + cross-body reach = weaker return.
4. Consistency. The slice has a higher in-percentage than the flat serve for most 3.5 players. 70% slice in is better than 50% flat in.
The flat serve is a weapon. The slice serve is a setup. In doubles, you want setup, not weapon.

💡 The "slice is the 50+ insurance" rule The slice serve is the 50+ doubles insurance policy. A 50+ player who serves 80% slice and 20% flat will have 70%+ first serves in, will pull the returner off the court, and will give the poacher a hittable ball. The slice is the joint-friendly, percentage-friendly, poacher-friendly serve.** Lead with the slice. Add the flat only on big points.

Serve slice là chính sách bảo hiểm đôi 50+. Người 50+ giao 80% slice và 20% flat sẽ có 70%+ serve 1 vào, sẽ kéo người trả rộng ra, và sẽ cho người cắt bóng để cắt. Slice là serve thân thiện khớp, thân thiện tỷ lệ, thân thiện cắt bóng. Dẫn đầu với slice. Thêm flat chỉ ở điểm lớn.

The Doubles Serve Mechanics — A Quick Refresher¶

🇺🇸
The mechanics of the doubles serve are the SAME as the singles serve. The Serve deep dive (6 chapters) covers the grip, trophy, throw, toss, rhythm, and contact in detail.
The 4 quick reminders for the doubles serve:
1. Grip: Continental (V to bevel #2, the volley grip). Same for all 4 patterns.
2. Trophy: elbow up, racket above the ear, palm on edge (NOT waiter's tray).
3. Throw: pronation, not wrist snap. The forearm rotates 180° from trophy to follow-through.
4. Toss: higher than feels right, slightly in front, brushing the ball at the peak.
What's NEW in this deep dive: the STRATEGY — where to aim, when to use which pattern, how to coordinate with the poacher.

Chapter 2 — Serve Wide: The 3.5 Default (Slice to the Backhand)¶

🇺🇸
The Serve Wide is the 3.5 doubles default and the 50+ doubles insurance. Slice the ball into the returner's backhand corner. Three things happen:
1. The returner is pulled wide off the court. They have to run to reach the ball.
2. The return is a defensive, loopy ball (the returner has no time to attack). The ball floats in the middle.
3. Your poacher has a hittable ball in the middle of the court. The poach wins.

🧠 Mental model The Serve Wide is a trap. You don't ace the returner — you PULL them off the court so the poacher can win the next shot. The serve is the setup. The poach is the point. Don't try to win the point on the serve. Win it on the next shot.**

Serve Rộng là bẫy. Anh không ace người trả — anh KÉO họ rộng ra để người cắt thắng cú tiếp. Serve là setup. Cú cắt là điểm. Đừng cố thắng điểm trên serve. Thắng ở cú tiếp.**

The 3-Step Serve Wide Sequence¶

1. AIM:    Pick the backhand corner (deuce corner for right-handers serving to the ad court)
2. SLICE:  Use slice (not flat), 70-80% power, brush the right side of the ball
3. CALL:  "Mine!" or "Poach!" to your partner BEFORE the serve — coordinate the poach

🎯 Part 1 — The Aim (Where to Land)¶

🇺🇸
The aim is the deuce corner of the service box (for right-handers serving to the ad court). Specifically:
- 2-3 feet inside the singles sideline (the wider the corner, the more the returner is pulled).
- 2-3 feet inside the baseline (deep target, the returner has to run forward AND wide).
The target is a 1-foot window, not a pinpoint. Anywhere in the deuce corner works.
Why the backhand? Most 3.5 returners have a weaker backhand return than forehand. The wide slice exploits the weakness AND pulls them off the court.

💡 The "backhand + corner" rule At 3.5, 80% of returns go back to the server's backhand (a 3.5 returner is more comfortable with the forehand). By serving wide to the backhand, you're forcing the returner to hit a defensive backhand OR a cross-court forehand that lands in the middle. Either way, the poach wins.**

Ở 3.5, 80% return đi về backhand server (người trả 3.5 thoải mái forehand hơn). Bằng cách giao rộng vào backhand, anh ép người trả đánh backhand phòng thủ HOẶC forehand chéo sân rơi giữa. Cả hai cách, cú cắt thắng.**

🎯 Part 2 — The Slice (How to Hit It)¶

🇺🇸
The slice serve has 3 specific mechanics that differ from the flat serve:
1. Contact the right side of the ball (for a right-hander serving to the ad court). The racket brushes the right side, the ball curves to the LEFT.
2. Toss slightly to the right (about 1 foot right of center, so the ball meets the racket on its right side).
3. Pronate through the contact (forearm rotates 180°), but the contact is at the right side of the ball, not the back.
The slice is NOT a slow serve. It's a serve with the same speed as the flat, but with a curve. The curve is what makes it special.
Power: 70-80% of full. The slice is not a max-effort serve. It's a controlled serve. The control is more important than the power.

🎯 The "right side brush" cue Imagine the ball is a clock face (looking down at the top of the ball). For a right-hander serving to the ad court, the slice contact is at 3 o'clock (the right side). The racket brushes from 7 o'clock to 3 o'clock — a 4-hour diagonal across the right side of the ball. The brush makes the ball spin to the LEFT.** That's the slice.

Hình dung bóng là mặt đồng hồ (nhìn xuống đỉnh bóng). Với người thuận phải giao vào sân ad, contact slice ở 3 giờ (phía phải). Vợt quét từ 7 giờ tới 3 giờ — đường chéo 4 giờ qua phía phải bóng. Quét làm bóng xoay sang TRÁI. Đó là slice.

🎯 Part 3 — The Call (Coordinating with the Poacher)¶

🇺🇸
The Serve Wide is a TEAM shot. You serve wide, your partner poaches. The coordination is everything.
The 3-step coordination:
1. Before the serve: "Poach!" or "Mine!" Shout to your partner. The poacher needs to know the serve is wide (so they know to poach to the middle, not to the wide side).
2. During the serve: watch the returner. The instant the returner makes contact, call "Now!" or "GO!" to the poacher. This is the "go signal" for the poach.
3. After the serve: stay back or move in. If the poach wins, you stay back (you're already there). If the poach misses, you cover the alley.
The call is non-verbal-friendly. At 50+ with hearing issues, use hand signals: fist behind back = "I'm poaching," open hand = "Stay."

🩺 The 50+ hearing tip At 50+, you might not hear your partner's "Poach!" call over the wind, the crowd, or your own serving rhythm. The fix: pre-serve hand signals. Before every serve, the poacher shows the server a hand signal: fist = "I'm poaching," open hand = "Stay," pointing to the alley = "Cover the alley." The signal is silent, visible, and 50+ reliable.**

Ở tuổi 50+, anh có thể không nghe tiếng "Cắt!" của partner qua gió, đám đông, hoặc nhịp serve của chính anh. Sửa: tín hiệu tay trước serve. Trước mỗi serve, người cắt cho server tín hiệu tay: nắm đấm = "tôi cắt," tay mở = "ở lại," chỉ biên ngoài = "che biên ngoài." Tín hiệu im, thấy được, và đáng tin ở 50+.**

Chapter 3 — Serve to the T: The Safest Target (Down the Middle)¶

🇺🇸
The Serve to the T is the safest doubles serve. Down the middle, into the service line, makes the returner hit UP at the poacher.
Why is the T the safest? Three reasons:
1. The shortest path to the service line. The T target is the closest spot in the service box to the net. The ball gets there fast, the returner has less reaction time.
2. The returner has to hit UP at the poacher. A T serve has a high arc (to clear the net), and the return goes UP into the poacher's wheelhouse.
3. The returner is jammed. A T serve pulls the returner toward the middle. They have to choose forehand or backhand quickly, and either choice is a defensive shot.
The T serve is the poacher's dream. The return floats UP, the poacher hits DOWN. The poach wins.

🧠 Mental model The T serve is a ceiling press. The ball goes UP at the returner (because of the high arc to clear the net), the return has to go UP again (because the returner is jammed), and the poacher is waiting at the lowest point of the parabola. The geometry guarantees a poach win.**

Serve T là trần ép. Bóng đi LÊN tới người trả (vì vòng cung cao qua lưới), return phải đi LÊN nữa (vì người trả bị ép), và người cắt chờ ở điểm thấp nhất của parabol. Hình học đảm bảo cú cắt thắng.**

When to Use the T Serve¶

Situation Tiếng Việt
The poacher is ready 🇺🇸 Your partner is at the net, hand signal = fist 🇻🇳 Partner ở lưới, tín hiệu tay = nắm đấm
The returner likes to cross-court 🇺🇸 The returner hits cross-court 90% of the time 🇻🇳 Người trả đánh chéo sân 90% thời gian
The returner is crowding the sideline 🇺🇸 The returner is standing 2-3 feet from the sideline 🇻🇳 Người trả đứng cách biên 60-90 cm
You need a safety serve 🇺🇸 You're at deuce, you need a high-percentage first serve 🇻🇳 Anh ở deuce, cần serve 1 tỷ lệ cao
All 4 conditions = T serve. The T is the "smart" serve when the poach is ready. Cả 4 điều kiện = serve T. T là serve "thông minh" khi cắt sẵn sàng.

💡 The "poacher's dream" insight The T serve is the poacher's dream because the geometry forces the return UP. A wide serve pulls the returner off the court (the return goes low and across). A body serve jams the returner (the return goes high but to the side). A T serve is the ONLY serve that forces the return UP into the poacher's wheelhouse. If your poacher is fast and confident, the T serve gives them the most hittable balls.**

Serve T là mơ ước người cắt vì hình học ép return LÊN. Serve rộng kéo người trả rộng (return đi thấp và ngang). Serve thân ép người trả (return đi cao nhưng sang bên). Serve T là serve DUY NHẤT ép return LÊN vào vùng ngon người cắt. Nếu người cắt anh nhanh và tự tin, serve T cho họ nhiều bóng để cắt nhất.**

The T Serve Aim (Where to Land)¶

🇺🇸
The T target is the center service line, about 1 foot inside the service line.
Specifically:
- 1 foot inside the service line (deep target, the returner has less time).
- 1 foot on the deuce side of the center line (for right-handers serving to the ad court) or 1 foot on the ad side (for serving to the deuce court).
The T target is a 1-foot window, not a pinpoint. Anywhere near the center line works.
The arc to the T is higher than the wide serve (because the T is closer to the net, the ball has to clear the net at a steeper angle). This high arc is what forces the return UP.

🎯 The "high arc" cue The T serve's high arc is a feature, not a bug. The high arc is what forces the return UP into the poacher's wheelhouse. A flat T serve would be a body serve (different pattern, different chapter). The T is high and deep, with the ball landing in the middle third of the service box.** That's the geometry that creates the poach opportunity.

Vòng cung cao của serve T là tính năng, không phải lỗi. Vòng cung cao là thứ ép return LÊN vào vùng ngon người cắt. Serve T phẳng sẽ là serve thân (mẫu khác, chương khác). T là cao và sâu, với bóng rơi phần ba giữa service box. Đó là hình học tạo cơ hội cắt.

The T Serve Mechanics¶

🇺🇸
The T serve can be a flat serve OR a slice serve. Both work.
- Flat T: maximum power, 90% of full, hit the back of the ball. Higher risk, higher reward.
- Slice T: slice with extra curve, 70-80% of full, brush the right side. Lower risk, slightly less reward.
At 50+, lead with the slice T. The flat T is for big points (3rd set, 5-4 in tiebreak).
The toss is different from the wide serve. For a T serve, toss the ball 1 foot LEFT of center (so the ball meets the racket on its left side, which makes the face close over the top of the ball).

💡 The "toss direction" rule Toss direction controls serve direction. Toss right → slice wide right. Toss center → flat down the middle. Toss left → kick up and to the left. The toss is the steering wheel of the serve.** A 3.5 player who can't control the toss direction can't control the serve direction.

Hướng tung kiểm soát hướng serve. Tung phải → slice rộng phải. Tung giữa → flat xuôi giữa. Tung trái → kick lên và sang trái. Tung là vô lăng của serve. Người 3.5 không kiểm soát được hướng tung thì không kiểm soát được hướng serve.

Chapter 4 — Serve to the Body: The Underused Weapon¶

🇺🇸
The body serve is the most underused serve in 3.5 doubles. Most players only know "wide" and "T." They don't know the body serve.
The body serve is devastating at 3.5. Why?
1. The returner can't swing. A body serve hits the returner at their torso. They can't extend their arms to swing. The return is a half-volley or a block.
2. The return is a slow floater. A jammed returner hits a slow, loopy ball. The poacher has all the time in the world.
3. The returner is in a defensive position. They can't attack from a body serve. The point is yours.
The body serve is "for the brave" at 3.5 because it's technically harder (the toss has to be precise, the contact has to be at the body). But once learned, it's a winning weapon.

🧠 Mental model The body serve is a jamming shot. The returner's arms are pinned to their body, their swing is short, their return is a float. The body serve is the closest thing to a guaranteed poach in doubles** — the return is always slow, always loopy, always hittable.

Serve thân là cú ép. Tay người trả bị ghim vào thân, vung ngắn, return là float. Serve thân là thứ gần nhất với cú cắt đảm bảo trong đôi — return luôn chậm, luôn vòng, luôn để cắt.

The returner likes to attack 🇺🇸 The returner has a big forehand or backhand and wants to attack 🇻🇳 Người trả có forehand hoặc backhand lớn và muốn tấn công

💡 The "crowding the line" insight Most 3.5 returners stand 2-3 feet from the sideline — they're preparing for the wide serve. A body serve catches them at their body. The returner who was ready for the wide serve is now caught at the body, no swing, no attack, slow float.** The poach wins.

Hầu hết người trả 3.5 đứng cách biên 60-90 cm — họ chuẩn bị cho serve rộng. Serve thân bắt họ ở thân. Người trả sẵn sàng cho serve rộng giờ bị bắt ở thân, không vung, không tấn công, float chậm. Cú cắt thắng.

The Body Serve Aim (Where to Land)¶

🇺🇸
The body serve hits the returner at their torso. Specifically:
- 1-2 feet inside the center service line (the opposite of the T, on the returner's body side).
- At the returner's torso height (the ball hits them at the belly button or chest).
The target is a 1-2 foot window. The ball should hit the returner, not pass them.
If the ball passes the returner, it's a fault. The body serve is a high-percentage target only if the returner is well-positioned. If the returner is far from the body line, the body serve becomes a fault.

⚠️ The 3.5 trap with the body serve The 3.5 player who tries the body serve often faults — the body serve has a tighter margin than the wide or T. The ball has to be at the body, not behind or in front. At 3.5, the body serve is "for the brave" because of the margin issue.** Lead with the wide serve, add the T serve, and only add the body serve once the wide and T are automatic.

Người 3.5 cố serve thân thường lỗi — serve thân có biên hẹp hơn serve rộng hoặc T. Bóng phải ở thân, không phía sau hoặc phía trước. Ở 3.5, serve thân là "dành cho người dũng cảm" vì vấn đề biên. Dẫn đầu với serve rộng, thêm serve T, và chỉ thêm serve thân khi serve rộng và T đã tự động.

Chapter 5 — Serve + Volley, Drills, Cues, Warnings, 50+ Check, Cheat Sheet¶

5A. Serve + Volley — The 50+ Offensive Option (For the Brave)¶

🇺🇸
The Serve + Volley is the most aggressive of the 4 patterns. You serve AND follow to the net. There's no partner — you are both the server AND the poacher.
When it works: when you don't have a partner (singles), when the returner's return is weak (singles or cutthroat doubles), when the body needs a change of pace.
The 3 steps:
1. Serve (any pattern, usually the wide slice for the 50+ default).
2. Split-step as the returner makes contact.
3. Sprint to the net at 70% (not 100% — save the knees). Arrive at the net, split-step again, volley.
At 50+, the Serve + Volley is "for the brave" because of the sprint load on the knees and the heart. Use it once or twice per set, not every point.

🩺 The 50+ Serve + Volley rule The Serve + Volley at 50+ has a hard limit: the sprint. The serve itself is joint-friendly (Continental + slice + 70% power = low load). But the 70% sprint to the net — that's the risk. At 50+, use Serve + Volley once or twice per set, on the points where the body feels good.** Your heart, your knees, and your back will thank you.

Serve + Volley ở 50+ có giới hạn cứng: chạy. Bản thân serve thân thiện khớp (Continental + slice + 70% lực = ít tải). Nhưng chạy 70% tới lưới — đó là rủi ro. Ở tuổi 50+, dùng Serve + Volley một hai lần mỗi set, ở điểm cơ thể cảm thấy tốt. Tim, gối, và lưng anh sẽ cảm ơn.

🛠️ The 5 Drills You Can Do This Week¶

Drill 1 — The "Aim Only" Practice (Solo, 5 min/day)¶

🇺🇸
Stand at the baseline. Place 3 cones (or use 3 towels) at the 3 serve targets:
- Cone 1: wide deuce corner (Serve Wide target).
- Cone 2: center service line, 1 ft inside (Serve T target).
- Cone 3: 1-2 ft inside center line, at returner's body (Serve Body target).
Hit 10 serves at each cone. Goal: 7/10 in the target window.

Drill 2 — The "Slice vs Flat" Comparison (Solo, 5 min/day)¶

🇺🇸
Hit 10 flat serves (aim for the wide corner). Then 10 slice serves (aim for the wide corner).
Notice the difference:
- Flat serve: the ball goes straight, fast, then drops. Less margin.
- Slice serve: the ball curves, dips, lands at the receiver's feet. More margin.
At 50+, the slice is the smarter default.

Drill 3 — The "Call the Poach" Practice (With Partner, 10 min)¶

🇺🇸
You serve. Your partner is at the net. Before every serve, your partner shows a hand signal:
- Fist = "I'm poaching."
- Open hand = "Stay."
- Point to alley = "Cover the alley."
You serve, you read the signal, you serve to the target that matches the signal.
Goal: 20 serves, 18 with correct signal interpretation.

Drill 4 — The "4 Patterns" Rotation (With Partner, 15 min)¶

🇺🇸
Play a practice set. Each service game, your partner calls out which of the 4 patterns to use (Wide, T, Body, or +Volley).
You execute the called pattern. If it's Wide, serve wide. If it's T, serve T.
Goal: 4 games, 1 of each pattern.
After each game, discuss: did the pattern work? Did the poach happen? What would you do differently?

Drill 5 — The "Toss Direction" Practice (Solo, 3 min/day)¶

🇺🇸
Stand at the baseline. Toss 10 balls, alternating between 3 toss positions:
- Toss 1: 1 foot right of center (slice wide).
- Toss 2: center (flat T).
- Toss 3: 1 foot left of center (kick or slice T).
Don't hit the balls. Just toss them and let them bounce. This is toss-only practice.
Goal: 30 tosses, 30 in the correct zone.

📄 Mental Checklist Before Each Doubles Serve¶

BEFORE THE SERVE (decision):
  ☐ Pick the pattern: WIDE (slice, default), T (poacher's dream), BODY (jam), or +VOLLEY (offensive)
  ☐ Pick the toss direction: right for slice wide, center for T, left for body
  ☐ Coordinate with poacher: hand signal (fist = poach, open hand = stay, point = alley)

THE SERVE (mechanics — same as Serve deep dive):
  ☐ Grip: Continental (V → bevel #2)
  ☐ Trophy: elbow up, racket above ear, palm on edge (NOT waiter's tray)
  ☐ Toss: higher than feels right, slightly in front, brush the ball at the peak
  ☐ Throw: pronation, not wrist snap
  ☐ Contact: full extension, racket head above the ball
  ☐ Power: 70-80% (smart doubles serve)
  ☐ Finish: over the non-dominant shoulder

AFTER THE SERVE (recovery):
  ☐ If Wide: stay back, watch the poach (your partner covers the middle)
  ☐ If T: stay back, watch the poach (your partner covers either side)
  ☐ If Body: stay back, watch the poach (your partner covers the open court)
  ☐ If +Volley: split-step → 70% sprint → split-step → volley

THE TEAM (always):
  ☐ Hand signal before serve
  ☐ "Now!" or "GO!" at the returner's contact
  ☐ "Move as a unit" — partner right, you left (after the poach)
  ☐ If the poach wins: stay back, you have the alley

🏁 END OF DEEP DIVE¶

Serve trong đôi không phải vũ khí. Serve là setup. 4 mẫu — Wide, T, Body, +Volley — là 4 cách setup khác nhau cho 4 tình huống khác nhau. Ở tuổi 50+, câu trả lời mặc định — 80% serve — là slice rộng vào góc backhand. Đó không phải lực. Đó là đặt bóng. Đó là phối hợp. Đó là tuổi thọ.

The serve in doubles is not a weapon. The serve is a setup. The 4 patterns — Wide, T, Body, +Volley — are 4 different setups for 4 different situations. At 50+, the default answer — 80% of serves — is the slice wide to the backhand corner. That's not power. That's placement. That's coordination. That's longevity.
