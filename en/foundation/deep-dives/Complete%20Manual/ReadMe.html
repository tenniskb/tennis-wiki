How to use v2¶

Step What Time
1 Open the v2 HTML in your browser —
2 Read front to back (Parts 1-13) 1.5 hours
3 Print the cheat sheet (Part 14, browser → print → "Background graphics" on, A4 duplex) 2 min
4 Pick ONE part to focus on for 2 weeks 2 weeks
5 Practice the drills in that part 15 min/day
6 Read the linked deep dive for that part 25 min
7 Move to the next part after 2 weeks repeat

Suggested 14-week rotation:

Week Part Drill focus
1-2 Part 1 (Foundations & Grip) Hammer pickup, tension scale, 2-second pause, grip-pressure pencil drill
3-4 Part 2 (Footwork) Bridge leg, split-step, 70% open stance
5-6 Part 3 (Groundstrokes) Read L3 → L2 → L1 → L4 forehand deep dives + Backhand full deep dive
7-8 Part 4 (Serve) Toss practice 20/day, full Serve deep dive
9 Part 5 (Volley) Wall volleys 5 min/day, full Volley deep dive
10 Part 6 (Return) Block + drive + chip drill, full Return deep dive
11 Part 7 (Strategy) 5-3-2 consistency drill
12 Part 8 (Doubles) Communication + formations, 4 doubles deep dives
13 Parts 9-10 (Lob & Slice) Sane Smash drill, slice 7-variations decision drill
14 Parts 11-13 (Injury, Pressure, Mental) Ice routine, pressure ladder, 4-step pre-point ritual

After 14 weeks, you'll have touched every Part. Then start the 14-week rotation again with a new Part focus.

Rebuilding the HTML¶

To regenerate the styled HTML from the v2 markdown source:

cd "C:\Users\Henry\Documents\New Tennis Knowledge\Deep Dives\Complete Manual"
python _build_html.py

The script requires markdown (already installed). It is self-contained — no other dependencies, no PDF stage, no system tools beyond Python stdlib.

v1 HTML is also preserved — to rebuild v1 instead, temporarily change the SRC_MD path in _build_html.py (or just keep both HTML files, which is what we recommend).

Key 3.5-level choices I made in v2¶

  • Added 3 new Parts instead of overstuffing existing Parts. Lob & Overhead, Slice Family, and Grip Pressure each got their own Part instead of being absorbed into Part 3 / Part 1. A 50+ player deserves dedicated space for the vertical game, the slice family, and the pressure dial.
  • Elevated Mental Game from Part 8 to Part 13. Mental Game was always important; in v1 it was a footnote. In v2 it's a full Part with the 5-phase match map, the 4-step pre-point ritual, the 5 pressure points, and the 4-action loss recovery. The body leads the mind deserves a full chapter.
  • 2-page cheat sheet instead of 1. 14 parts won't fit on one page. Page 1 covers Parts 1-7 (singles + foundation). Page 2 covers Parts 8-14 (doubles + lob + slice + injury + pressure + mental + master cue).
  • Stayed at the map level — ~90 KB, not 200 KB. The 22 deep dives have the detail; the manual has the index.
  • Skipped frame counts and joint angles — those belong at 4.5+. The manual is for playing, not for academic study.
  • Translated biomechanics into body cues — "gravity serve" not "kinematic chain with peak angular velocity in distal segments."
  • Made the 50+ adjustments pervasive — every part has a 50+ block. The 50+ reality is the filter for every cue.
  • Embedded the 2-page cheat sheet at the end — fits the Surrey bag when folded.
  • Cross-linked to ALL 22 deep dives + 4 foundational files — when the manual is too short, the source notes and deep dives are right there.
  • Web-only output — no PDF pipeline. The HTML version is printable from any browser; no extra build step, no extra binary, no extra dependency to install.
  • Preserved v1 unchanged — series parameters, voice, Part 8 mental section are all kept for archival continuity.

The most important line from v2¶

"Tennis is a whip sport, not a hammer sport. And you are playing a 20-year game."

Every choice you make — grip, swing, shoes, warmup, recovery, mindset — is either investing in 20 more years on court, or borrowing from your knees. v2 is a 20-year insurance policy with 22 deep dives, 2 cheat-sheet pages, and 14 Parts of map-level guidance.
