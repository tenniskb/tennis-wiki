Agassi Return Mastery Program¶

Overview¶

This training program is designed to develop a return of serve modeled after Andre Agassi, widely regarded as having one of the greatest return games in tennis history. The program focuses on developing exceptional reaction time, anticipation, positioning, and the ability to turn defense into offense—hallmarks of Agassi's return that helped him break down some of the greatest servers of all time.

Program Overview¶

  • Duration: 10 weeks (can be adjusted based on athlete level)
  • Frequency: 3-4 sessions per week
  • Session Length: 60-90 minutes
  • Equipment Needed: Tennis court, balls, ball machine or serving partner, targets (cones, zones), agility ladder, reaction balls, video analysis equipment
  • Prerequisites: Proper grip (continental or eastern forehand grip for forehand return, continental or two-handed backhand grip for backhand return), basic split step and footwork fundamentals

Phase 1: Foundation Weeks (Weeks 1-2)¶

Goals: Develop proper stance, split timing, and basic direction control¶

Technical Work¶

  • Split Step Timing Drills (15 minutes daily)
  • Shadow split steps to serve motion (no ball) - focus on timing the split with opponent's contact
  • Jump rope with emphasis on quick feet and timing

  • Reaction ball drills for hand-eye coordination and reaction time

  • Positioning and Court Awareness (20 minutes)

  • Practice standing in optimal return position (baseline for second serve, inside baseline for weak first serve)
  • Diagonal movement patterns to cover wide serves

  • Shadow returns to different serve locations (wide, body, T)

  • Basic Direction Control (15 minutes)

  • Crosscourt returns from forehand and backhand sides
  • Down-the-line returns from forehand and backhand sides
  • Focus on clearing the net height and depth (aiming for 3-6 feet over baseline)

Physical Conditioning¶

  • Reaction and Agility
  • Ladder drills: in-and-out, lateral shuffles, ickey shuffles (4 sets of 30 seconds)
  • Cone drills: T-drill, pro-agility shuffle (3 sets each direction)

  • Ball drop reaction drills (partner drops ball, you catch after one bounce) - 3 sets of 20

  • Lower Body Explosiveness

  • Squat jumps: 3 sets of 10
  • Lateral bounds: 3 sets of 15 each side

  • Single-leg hops: 3 sets of 20 each leg

  • Core and Rotational Strength

  • Medicine ball rotational throws: 3 sets of 15 each side
  • Cable woodchoppers: 3 sets of 12 each side
  • Plank with shoulder taps: 3 sets of 30 seconds

Practice Application¶

  • Return to Targets (20 minutes)
  • Deuce court: crosscourt angle, down-the-line, middle
  • Ad court: crosscourt angle, down-the-line, middle
  • Use cones or zones to aim for
  • Start with second serves, progress to first serves

Week 1-2 Schedule Example¶

  • Monday: Technique + Agility (90 min)
  • Wednesday: Positioning + Plyometrics (75 min)
  • Friday: Direction Control + Core (90 min)
  • Sunday: Match Play Simulation (60 min) - focus on returning only

Phase 2: Reaction and Anticipation Development (Weeks 3-4)¶

Goals: Improve serve recognition, reduce reaction time, develop proactive positioning¶

Technical Work¶

  • Serve Recognition Drills (20 minutes)
  • Video analysis of serve toss patterns (pronation, grip, shoulder angle)
  • Live serving practice: call out serve type (flat, slice, kick) before ball contact

  • Machine or partner serving to different locations, you call out location before bounce

  • Proactive Movement Patterns (15 minutes)

  • "Step in" moves for second serves (taking the ball on the rise)
  • Diagonal backward steps for wide serves

  • Crossover steps for extreme wide serves

  • Return Positioning Strategies (10 minutes)

  • When to stand in (second serve, weak first serve)
  • When to stay back (strong first serve, big server)
  • How to adjust based on server's tendencies

Physical Conditioning¶

  • Reaction Speed
  • Light-based reaction systems (if available) or partner-led light signals
  • Shuffle to ball drop (random direction)

  • 5-10-5 pro agility with visual cue

  • Explosive First Step

  • Resisted sprints with bands (10 yards, 4 sets)
  • Falling starts to sprint (3 sets of 5 each direction)

  • Box drill with explosive exits (4 corners)

  • Rotational Power

  • Med ball slams: 3 sets of 15
  • Rotational cable pushes: 3 sets of 12 each side
  • Russian twists with weight: 3 sets of 20

Practice Application¶

  • Return and Attack (20 minutes)
  • Focus on taking short serves and attacking immediately
  • Approach shots off weak returns

  • Practice volley after aggressive return

  • Consistency Under Pressure (15 minutes)

  • Target depth: aim for last 3 feet of court
  • Consecutive in-play returns goal (e.g., 20 in a row)
  • Simulate break points: must make return to win point

Week 3-4 Schedule Example¶

  • Tuesday: Reaction + Explosiveness (90 min)
  • Thursday: Recognition + Positioning (75 min)
  • Saturday: Attacking Returns + Consistency (90 min)
  • Sunday: Match Play Simulation (60 min) - focus on aggressive second serve returns

Phase 3: Pressure and Tactical Integration (Weeks 5-6)¶

Goals: Perform under match pressure, develop return strategies based on server tendencies¶

Technical Work¶

  • Pattern Recognition and Anticipation (20 minutes)
  • Study opponent's serving patterns (deuce vs ad, first vs second serve)
  • Develop "if-then" plans based on serve location and type

  • Practice disguising your return intentions until last moment

  • Advanced Return Techniques (15 minutes)

  • Block return for heavy serves (minimal backswing)
  • Chip and charge for second serves

  • Swinging volley off high bouncing serves

  • Mental Routines (10 minutes)

  • Pre-serve routine: feet positioning, grip adjustment, focus point
  • Between-points routines to stay focused
  • Managing frustration on missed returns

Physical Conditioning¶

  • Repeat Sprint Ability
  • 300-yard shuttles (6x50 yards) with short rest
  • Tabata intervals: 20 seconds on/10 seconds off for 4 minutes (agility ladder or sprints)

  • Hill sprints: 8x20 seconds uphill walk down

  • Core Endurance

  • Plank variations: front, side, reverse (3 sets of 45 seconds each)
  • Hanging knee raises: 3 sets of 20

  • Pallof press: 3 sets of 15 each side

  • Injury Prevention

  • Eccentric heel drops for Achilles: 3 sets of 15
  • External rotations with bands: 3 sets of 20
  • Scapular wall slides: 3 sets of 15

Practice Application¶

  • Match Simulation Returns (25 minutes)
  • Play return games: return serve only, server gets point if you miss return
  • Serve and return sets: first to 6 games, but only return points count

  • Practice returning under different score situations (love-40, deuce, ad-in/out)

  • Strategic Application (15 minutes)

  • Practice specific return strategies against different server types:
    • Big server: block deep, look for second serve to attack
    • Spin specialist: take on the rise, use pace
    • Lefty serve: adjust positioning and swing path

Week 5-6 Schedule Example¶

  • Monday: Pattern Recognition + Repeat Sprints (90 min)
  • Wednesday: Advanced Techniques + Core (75 min)
  • Friday: Match Simulations + Strategy (90 min)
  • Sunday: Practice Match (90 min) - focus on implementing return game plan

Phase 4: Match Play Integration and Maintenance (Weeks 7-10)¶

Goals: Integrate return game into overall match strategy, maintain and refine skills¶

Technical Work¶

  • Return in Context of Point Construction (20 minutes)
  • Practice using return to set up forehand
  • Develop patterns: return crosscourt, move to net, volley

  • Practice using return to neutralize and restart point

  • Advanced Tactical Situations (15 minutes)

  • Returning serve-and-volley opponents
  • Returning in doubles (positioning, communication)

  • Returning second serves with aggression and variety

  • Video Analysis and Self-Review (10 minutes)

  • Review match footage of your returns
  • Compare to Agassi's technique and decision making
  • Identify one area to improve each week

Physical Conditioning¶

  • Maintenance Phase
  • Reduce volume, maintain intensity
  • Focus on injury prevention and recovery

  • Continue agility and reaction work at reduced volume

  • Peak Performance Preparation

  • Taper training volume in final week before competition
  • Maintain sharpness with short, focused sessions
  • Prioritize sleep, nutrition, and hydration

Practice Application¶

  • Full Match Play (25 minutes)
  • Play practice sets and matches
  • Focus on implementing return game plan

  • Track return statistics: % made, % aggressive points won, break points converted

  • Cool-down and Reflection (10 minutes)

  • Static stretching for legs, shoulders, back
  • Brief journaling on what worked and what to improve

Week 7-10 Schedule Example¶

  • Mondays: Technique + Video Analysis (75 min)
  • Wednesdays: Tactical Situations + Maintenance Fitness (75 min)
  • Fridays: Match Play (90 min)
  • Sundays: Light Session + Recovery (60 min) or Match Play

Key Drills Library¶

Split Step and Reaction¶

  1. Shadow Split to Serve: Practice split step timing to shadow serve motion
  2. Toss Call: Partner serves, you call out toss location before contact
  3. Reaction Ball Shuffle: Shuffle laterally, catch reaction ball after bounce
  4. Light Sprint to Cone: React to visual cue, sprint to designated cone

Positioning and Movement¶

  1. Baseline to Target: Start at baseline, move to touch cone, recover to base
  2. Diagonal Recovery: Hit shadow return, recover to ideal position for next shot
  3. In-and-Out Steps: Practice moving forward for short serves, back for deep
  4. Shadow Return and Recover: Full motion return, then recover to center

Direction and Depth Control¶

  1. Cone Alley: Aim returns between two cones placed at baseline
  2. Depth Ladder: Hit to progressively deeper zones (short, medium, deep)
  3. Crosscourt/Down-the-Line Alternation: Alternate directions with each shot
  4. Target Pyramid: Hit to specific targets in sequence (wide, body, T, wide)

Pressure and Match Play¶

  1. Return Game: Play games where only return points count
  2. Break Point Simulation: Serve at 0-40, you must break to win game
  3. Consistency Challenge: See how many returns you can make in a row
  4. Score Situation Returns: Practice returning at specific scores (30-40, ad-in, etc.)

Physical Conditioning Guidelines¶

Agility and Reaction Focus¶

  • Frequency: 3-4 times per week
  • Intensity: High effort, short duration (10-20 seconds per bout)
  • Recovery: Full recovery between bouts (40-60 seconds rest)
  • Progression: Increase complexity before increasing duration

Strength and Power Focus¶

  • Frequency: 2-3 times per week
  • Intensity: Moderate to high (60-85% 1RM equivalent)
  • Volume: 2-4 sets of 8-15 repetitions
  • Progression: Increase resistance before increasing volume

Endurance Focus¶

  • Frequency: 2 times per week
  • Type: Interval-based (mimics tennis work-rest ratios)
  • Example: 30 seconds high intensity/90 seconds active recovery x 8-10

Evaluation and Progress Tracking¶

Weekly Metrics to Track¶

  1. Return Percentage: % of serves returned in play (overall, first serve, second serve)
  2. Aggressive Return Percentage: % of returns that are offensive (approach shot, winner, forced error)
  3. Break Points Converted: % of break point opportunities won
  4. Return Points Won: % of points won when returning serve
  5. Unforced Errors on Return: % of return errors that are unforced

Monthly Assessment¶

  1. Video Analysis: Compare technique to baseline video
  2. Match Statistics: Track return performance in practice matches
  3. Physical Testing:
  4. 10-yard sprint time
  5. Pro-agility time
  6. Vertical jump height
  7. Medicine ball throw distance
  8. Subjective Rating: Rate your confidence returning serve (1-10 scale)

Adaptation for Different Levels¶

Beginner Focus (NTRP 2.5-3.5)¶

  • Weeks 1-2: Extended focus on split step and basic positioning
  • Weeks 3-4: Slower pace serves, emphasis on making contact
  • Weeks 5-6: Introduction to direction control with larger targets
  • Weeks 7-10: Game-based situations, focus on consistency over aggression

Intermediate Focus (NTRP 4.0-4.5)¶

  • Standard progression as outlined above
  • Emphasis: Developing aggressive return options
  • Adjustment: Increase serve speed gradually

Advanced Focus (NTRP 5.0+)¶

  • Accelerated timeline: 6-8 week program possible
  • Emphasis: Tactical variations and pressure performance
  • Adjustment: Incorporate opponent-specific strategies and scouting

Senior/Injury-Return Focus¶

  • Reduced Impact: Substitute jumping with step movements
  • Increased Recovery: Longer rest intervals, additional recovery days
  • Focus: Precision and placement over explosive movement
  • Equipment: Use of softer balls, smaller court if needed

Integration with Overall Game¶

  • Serve Game: Use strong return to break serve and reduce pressure on your own service games
  • Baseline Play: Develop transitions from return to baseline rallies
  • Net Play: Practice approaching the net after strong returns
  • Mental Game: Develop routines to stay focused and positive during return games

Coach's Notes¶

  • Individualization: Adjust based on player's age, physique, and return style (blocker vs. puncher)
  • Video Feedback: Use delayed video or live feedback for immediate correction
  • Patience: Return improvements take consistent practice; expect 6-8 weeks for noticeable changes
  • Fun and Engagement: Incorporate games and challenges to maintain motivation
  • Holistic Development: Return is crucial but ensure balanced development of all skills

Safety Considerations¶

  • Warm-up: Always complete dynamic warm-up before intense reaction work
  • Cool-down: Static stretching for hips, legs, shoulders, and back after sessions
  • Hydration: Maintain proper fluid intake, especially during intense sessions
  • Surface: Be aware of court conditions; slippery surfaces increase injury risk
  • Equipment: Use appropriate footwear with good lateral support

References & Resources¶

  • Video Study: Andre Agassi return highlights (1990s US Open, Australian Open)
  • Books: "Andre Agassi: Open" (autobiography), tennis technique texts
  • Coaching Aids: USPTA/PTR return of serve development materials
  • Technology: Reaction lights, high-speed cameras for analysis if available

Program developed based on analysis of Andre Agassi's return technique, training methodologies, and match performance data. Adapt volume, intensity, and focus based on individual athlete needs, age, and competitive level. Consult with qualified tennis coach and fitness professional before beginning any intensive training program.
