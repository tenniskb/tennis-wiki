Andre Agassi: The Baseline Revolutionary¶

Overview¶

  • Full Name: Andre Kirk Agassi
  • Born: April 29, 1970, Las Vegas, Nevada, USA
  • Turned Pro: 1986
  • Retired: 2006 (after US Open 2006)
  • Career Prize Money: $31,152,975
  • Height: 5'11" (180 cm)
  • Plays: Right-handed (two-handed backhand)
  • Career Titles: 60 singles titles
  • Grand Slam Singles Titles: 8 (4 Australian Open, 2 US Open, 1 Wimbledon, 1 French Open)
  • Career High Ranking: World No. 1 (April 10, 1995)
  • Weeks at No. 1: 101
  • Olympic Gold Medal: Men's Singles (1996 Atlanta Olympics)

Playing Style & Strengths¶

The Return of Serve¶

  • Considered the Greatest Returner in History: Ability to neutralize even the best serves
  • Early Preparation: Took the ball extremely early, often inside the baseline
  • Return Placement: Could hit returns deep, short, or angled with equal precision
  • Second Serve Destruction: Frequently broke serve on second serves

Groundstrokes¶

  • Forehand: Flat, aggressive stroke with minimal topspin, hit early and hard
  • Backhand: Two-handed backhand that was both defensive and offensive weapon
  • Shot Making: Exceptional ability to hit winners from defensive positions
  • Consistency: Remarkable ability to keep the ball in play under pressure

Movement & Court Coverage¶

  • Speed: Exceptional court coverage, particularly laterally
  • Sliding: Mastered sliding on hard courts to reach wide balls
  • Recovery: Quick recovery position after hitting shots
  • Anticipation: Advanced court sense and pattern recognition

Mental & Competitive Traits¶

  • Fighter Mentality: Famous for never giving up, even when down
  • Pressure Performer: Often played best in big moments
  • Emotional Intensity: Visible emotions that fueled his competitiveness
  • Adaptability: Continually evolved his game throughout his career

Career Highlights¶

Grand Slam Breakdown¶

  • Australian Open: 4 titles (1995, 2000, 2001, 2003) - Open Era record at retirement
  • French Open: 1 title (1999) - Completed career Grand Slam
  • Wimbledon: 1 title (1992) - Only Wimbledon win came early in career
  • US Open: 2 titles (1994, 1999)

Career Grand Slam¶

  • Achieved: 1999 French Open victory
  • Historical Significance: First male player to win all four Slams on three different surfaces (hard, clay, grass)
  • Note: Only player to achieve Career Golden Slam (all 4 Slams + Olympic Gold)

Other Notable Achievements¶

  • Olympic Gold Medal: 1996 Atlanta Olympics (singles)
  • Year-End No. 1: 1999
  • Masters Cup/ATP Finals: 1990 champion
  • Davis Cup: Member of winning US teams (1990, 1992, 1995)
  • Career Match Record: 870-274 (.760 winning percentage)

Rivalries¶

Pete Sampras (14-20)¶

  • Grand Slam Finals: 5 (US Open '90, '95; Wimbledon '99; Australian Open '00, '01) - Won 1
  • Style Contrast: Baseline aggression vs. serve-and-volley
  • Memorable Matches: 2001 US Open QF (4-hour epic considered one of greatest ever), 1995 US Open Final (4 sets), 1999 Wimbledon Final (straight sets)

Other Notable Rivalries¶

  • Michael Chang: 22-15 Agassi (notable for psychological battles)
  • Jim Courier: 20-13 Agassi
  • Patrick Rafter: 10-6 Agassi (including 2000 Australian Open Final in 5 sets)

Legacy & Impact¶

On the Game¶

  • Return Revolution: Redefined importance of return of serve as offensive weapon
  • Baseline Power: Showed aggressive baseline game could win all Slams
  • Global Ambassador: Helped globalize tennis through charisma and marketability
  • Fashion Influence: Famous for colorful attire, denim shorts, and long hair (early career)
  • Longevity Model: Won Slams in teens, 20s, and 30s

Playing Style Influence¶

  • Return Technique: Modern returners study his early preparation and aggression
  • Two-Handed Backhand: Benchmark for consistency and versatility
  • Half-Volley: Mastered the difficult half-volley as both defensive and offensive tool
  • Court Positioning: Demonstrated effectiveness of taking ball early and inside baseline

Off-Court¶

  • Philanthropy: Founded Andre Agassi College Preparatory Academy in Las Vegas
  • Autobiography: "Open" (2009) - critically acclaimed, brutally honest memoir
  • Business Ventures: Various endorsements and business interests
  • Cultural Icon: Transcended tennis to become mainstream celebrity

Training & Technique Insights¶

Return of Serve Mastery¶

  1. Early Preparation: Begins preparation as opponent tosses ball
  2. Compact Swing: Short backswing to meet ball early
  3. Weight Transfer: Steps into return, transferring weight forward
  4. Target Selection: Varies returns based on serve type and placement
  5. Second Serve Aggression: Specifically practices attacking second serves

Groundstroke Technique¶

  1. Grip Selection: Eastern forehand grip for flat power, continental for slice
  2. Stance Variety: Uses open, semi-open, and closed stances situationally
  3. Hip Rotation: Generates power through hip and shoulder rotation
  4. Follow-Through Control: Varies follow-through for depth, angle, and spin control

Mental & Emotional Management¶

  1. Routine Development: Consistent pre-point routines to manage nerves
  2. Emotional Channeling: Uses emotions as fuel rather than distraction
  3. Problem-Solving Mindset: Views challenges as puzzles to solve
  4. Resilience Training: Practices staying positive during losing streaks

Fitness & Conditioning¶

  1. Agility Ladder: Extensive footwork drills for court coverage
  2. Core Strength: Emphasis on rotational power for groundstrokes
  3. Reaction Training: Light-based and partner drills for reflex improvement
  4. Endurance Work: Interval training to simulate point patterns

Famous Matches & Moments¶

1995 US Open Final vs. Pete Sampras¶

  • Lost in 4 sets (6-4, 6-3, 4-6, 7-5)
  • Showcased incredible returning against Sampras' serve
  • Match highlighted their contrasting styles perfectly

1999 French Open Final vs. Andrei Medvedev¶

  • Won in 5 sets (1-6, 2-6, 6-4, 6-3, 6-4)
  • Came back from two sets down to complete Career Grand Slam
  • Emotional victory that cemented his legacy

2001 US Open Quarterfinal vs. Pete Sampras¶

  • Lost in 4 sets (6-7, 7-6, 7-6, 7-6) - all sets went to tiebreak
  • 4-hour marathon considered one of greatest matches ever played
  • Featured incredible shot-making from both players

1992 Wimbledon Final vs. Goran Ivanisevic¶

  • Won in 5 sets (6-7, 6-4, 6-4, 1-6, 6-4)
  • Only Wimbledon title, showcased versatility on grass
  • Notable for his all-white outfit departure from usual colorful attire

1996 Olympic Gold Medal Match¶

  • Defeated Sergi Bruguera 6-2, 6-3, 6-1
  • Dominant performance to win America's first men's tennis gold since 1924

Fun Facts & Trivia¶

  • Famous for his image-conscious early career ("Image is everything" campaign)
  • Famous for donating his prize money to charity after certain victories
  • Known for his philanthropic work in education through his Academy
  • Married to fellow tennis legend Steffi Graf (married 2001)
  • His autobiography "Open" revealed he hated tennis for much of his career
  • Known for his charitable work, particularly in education for underprivileged youth

Notable Quotes¶

"I hate tennis, I love tennis. I hate it with a passion, but I love it with a passion."

"The scoreboard can be cruel, but it's always honest."

"You have to believe in yourself when no one else does - that's what makes you a winner."

"The most important thing is to try and inspire people so that they can be great in whatever they want to do."

"Life doesn't give us purpose. We give life purpose."

Training Regimen Insights¶

  • Early Career: Intense, demanding regimen designed by his father Mike
  • Later Years: More balanced approach focusing on longevity and injury prevention
  • Return Practice: Hundreds of returns daily, varying serve types and speeds
  • Footwork Drills: Extensive ladder work, cone drills, and shadow tennis
  • Mental Training: Worked with sports psychologists on emotional management
  • Recovery Focus: Increased emphasis on rest, nutrition, and recovery as he aged
