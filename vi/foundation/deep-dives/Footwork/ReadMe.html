This is the first non-stroke deep dive. Footwork is the silent foundation under every shot — the topic that turns all 6 stroke deep-dives from "I know the technique" into "I can actually use it in a trận."
## Key insights drawn from your source material
1. The "Đạp Xe" / Idling principle — your giáo trình's "Luôn giữ gót chân sau nhấc nhẹ" became the central cue. Body idles like an engine, never parks like a car.
2. The 4 named moves — pulled your giáo trình's table (Lateral Hop / Drop Step / Cross-Over / Recovery) and built an entire chapter around them, in order of difficulty.
3. The 300ms split-step gift — your giáo trình said "thêm 300ms quý báu." I made that the central hook of Chapter 3.
4. The Cross-Over Cẩn thận — your "high-injury move" warning was transformed into a 50+ rule: use it as a last resort, not a first choice.
5. Tai chi integration — Thường Tùng (always loose/ready), Cung Bộ (bow tư thế), Tụ Bộ (drop step), Hoàn Thủ (return to center). Each maps to a di chuyển principle and reinforces the cross-series Giọng nói.
## Key 3.5-level choices I made
- Skipped the GRF / motor unit recruitment biomechanics from your giáo trình — those belong at 4.5+, would overwhelm a 3.5
- Translated "staggered tư thế" into a body image — "your feet are the bow and string, not a square box"
- Added the "Hunter Stance" cue — forward lean at the ankles, never the waist, with the "bowl of water in your pelvis" test for back safety
- Added the Triangle Drill — a điểm-simulation bài tập that chains all four moves in sequence
- Made the 50+ rule about longevity — "smart beats fast, always" — not just performance but joint protection
## The most important line from this deep dive
> "You're playing a 20-year sport. Footwork is your longevity insurance policy. Buy it now."
A player who starts 300ms early with small steps beats a player who starts 800ms late with a sprint. The early starter's heart rate stays low, the joints stay healthy, and the trận lasts 3 hours instead of 90 minutes.
